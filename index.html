Pre-Festival

📊 Why India Underperformed at Cannes 2026

After two days of awards, India has won just 5 Lions with zero Gold — its worst start in at least five years. By comparison, India had 12 metals including 2 Golds at the same stage in 2025, and 9 metals with 1 Gold in 2024. Here's what the industry says went wrong.
2026: 5 Lions, 0 Gold · 2025 same point: 12 Lions, 2 Gold · 2024 same point: 9 Lions, 1 Gold

📉 Sharp Drop in Entries

India sent only 676 entries this year, down 31.2% from 982 in 2025 — outpacing the global decline of 25.5%. Fewer entries means fewer chances to shortlist, let alone win. India's share of global submissions slipped from ~3.7% to ~3.4%.

💸 Awards ROI Scrutiny

Industry observers attribute the entry decline to agencies and marketers taking a harder look at awards-related spending — entry fees, travel budgets, delegation sizes and overall ROI from international festivals. The mood has shifted to quality over volume.

🎯 Narrow Category Focus

India had zero shortlists in Digital Craft, PR, Industry Craft, Entertainment (all four Lions), Social & Creator, Creative B2B, Creative Business Transformation, Luxury, Creative Effectiveness and Creative Data — categories where India historically punched above its weight.

🔄 Low Conversion Rate

India converted only 5 of its 24 shortlists into metals — a ~21% conversion rate. Several shortlisted campaigns in Direct, Media, Design and Film Craft failed to make it onto the winners' podium, despite being considered strong contenders going in.

🤔 Underconfidence on Global Stage

OMD India's Anisha Iyer noted "a slight undertone of underconfidence in India" — suggesting Indian agencies may not be fully backing their best ideas for the most competitive global categories, or are not entering the right work in the right categories.

📌 No Gold in Sight (Yet)

With Day 4 awards still to come in Brand Experience & Activation, Creative Commerce and Creative Strategy, India has a chance to salvage higher-value metals. But the structural gaps — no Grand Prix contenders, no Gold shortlists yet — reflect a deeper shift in India's Cannes strategy.
