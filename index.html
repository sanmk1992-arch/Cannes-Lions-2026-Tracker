📊 Why India Underperformed at Cannes 2026

India won just 5 Lions with zero Gold — its worst finish in at least five years. By comparison, India had 32 Lions including 1 Grand Prix in 2025. Here's what went wrong.
2026: 5 · 2025: 32 · 2024: ~18 · 2023: ~15 · 2022: ~22

📉 Entry Collapse

India sent only 676 entries — down 31.2% from 982 in 2025, outpacing the global 25% decline. Fewer entries = fewer shortlists = fewer chances to win.

💸 ROI Scrutiny

Agencies and marketers took a harder look at entry fees, travel costs and delegation budgets. The mood shifted to quality over volume — but the quality didn't match either.

🎯 Category Blind Spots

India had zero shortlists in Digital Craft, PR, Industry Craft, Entertainment (all four), Social & Creator, Creative B2B, Business Transformation, Luxury and Creative Effectiveness.

🔄 Poor Conversion

India converted only 5 of 24 shortlists to metals — a ~21% hit rate. Campaigns in Direct, Media, Design, Creative Strategy and Film Craft all failed to crack the winners' circle.

🤔 Underconfidence

OMD India's Anisha Iyer called out "a slight undertone of underconfidence" — suggesting India may not be entering the right work in the right categories on the global stage.

🌏 Asia Context

Japan (~15 Lions), China (~10) and Singapore (~8) all outperformed India in 2026. India's drop from 32 → 5 Lions is the steepest decline of any major Asian market year-on-year.
